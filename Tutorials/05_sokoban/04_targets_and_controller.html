Phase 3: Targets and Game Controller

Let's finish the game by adding target spots, visual feedback, and a controller with instructions!

Create the Target and Crate-OK Sprites

  1. Create a new sprite: spr_target (32×32 pixels)
  2. Draw a red or yellow X, diamond, or circle to mark the target spot
  3. Import from Tutorials/05_sokoban/assets/ if you prefer
  4. Press Ctrl+S to save
  1. Create a new sprite: spr_crate_ok (32×32 pixels)
  2. Draw a green version of your crate — this shows a correctly placed crate
  3. Press Ctrl+S to save

Create the Target Object

  1. Right-click on Objects → Create New Object
  2. Name it obj_target, set its sprite to spr_target
  3. Do NOT check the Solid checkbox — the player and crates must be able to walk over targets
Why not solid? Targets are floor markers. The player walks over them and crates rest on top of them. If targets were solid, nothing could reach them!

Program the Crate: Visual Feedback

  1. Double-click obj_crate to open the Object Editor
  2. Click the Blockly tab
  3. Add a Step event with an if/else check:
Step
If colliding with obj_target
Set sprite to spr_crate_ok
Else
Set sprite to spr_crate

Find Step in the Events category. Then find Check Collision in the Control category and type obj_target. Snap a Set Sprite block (from Instance) inside for the "then" case with spr_crate_ok. Add an Else section with another Set Sprite block for spr_crate.

Click Apply to Events to save the crate's blocks.

Visual Feedback: When a crate is on a target, it turns green. When pushed off, it returns to its normal color. This tells the player which crates are correctly placed!

Create the Game Controller

  1. Right-click on Objects → Create New Object
  2. Name it obj_controller
  3. No sprite is needed for this object

Program the Controller: Draw Instructions

  1. Double-click obj_controller to open the Object Editor
  2. Click the Blockly tab
  3. Add a Draw event to show instructions:
Draw
Draw Text "Push crates onto targets!" at 10, 10

Find Draw in the Events category. Then find Draw Text in the Drawing category. Type Push crates onto targets! as the text, and set X to 10, Y to 10.

Program the Controller: Restart Level

Keyboard Press: R
Restart Room

Find Keyboard Press in Events and select R. Then find Restart Room in the Room category. This lets the player reset the puzzle if they get stuck!

  1. You should have 2 event blocks (Draw + Keyboard Press R)
  2. Click Apply to Events to save

Update the Room

  1. Open room_sokoban
  2. Place obj_target instances where you want crates to end up
  3. Make sure the number of targets equals the number of crates
  4. Place obj_controller anywhere in the room (it has no sprite, so it won't be visible)
Important: Number of crates MUST equal number of targets! If you have 2 crates, place exactly 2 targets.

Example Level Layout

W W W W W W W W W W
W . . . . . . . . W
W . P . . . C . . W
W . . W W . . . . W
W . . W T . . C . W
W . . . . . W W . W
W . T . . . . . . W
W . . . . . . . . W
W . . . . . . . . W
W W W W W W W W W W

W = Wall     T = Target
P = Player   C = Crate
. = Empty
Tip: Start with a simple puzzle! Make sure the player can actually solve it by mentally tracing a path before placing the objects.

Test Your Game!

Press F5 to play!

  • Use the arrow keys to walk and push crates
  • Push each crate onto a target spot — it turns green!
  • When all crates are green, you've solved the puzzle
  • Press R to restart the level if you get stuck
  • The instructions appear at the top of the screen

Congratulations! Your Sokoban game is complete!

Challenge Ideas

  • Harder levels: Create new rooms with more crates and tighter corridors
  • Move counter: Add a variable to count how many moves the player makes
  • Multiple rooms: Create several rooms with increasing difficulty and use Go to next room when all crates are placed
  • Undo feature: Store the previous position and let the player press U to undo the last move
